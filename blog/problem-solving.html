Problem-solving techniques

  • Pseudocode:

    • I feel ok with this one. I think it is still quite complicated to imagine what the solution will be to actually write down what I have to do.

  • Trying something:

    • Definitely my go to, I realise that I always try to solve the problem myself for a good 10/15 min before I do any other kind of search.

  • Rubber ducky method:

    • I find this method very fun and sometimes it does work, I cant say I remember to do that all the time but when I do it is helpful.

  • Reading error messages:

    • I use Reading error messages quiet a lot and I find it very easy to understand and resourceful at times.

  • Console.logging:

    • Not what I use the most I have to say, I still don’t fell very confident about using console.log even though I know I should be using it much more.

  • Googling:

    • Googling is one of the first things I’ll do when I actually have to look somewhere else for a solution.

  • Asking your peers for help:

    • I don’t think I feel confident about asking for help, most of the times I think I am going to look dumb on a problem. That would be my last resource.

  • Asking coaches for help:

    • I am trying to get more confortable asking coaches for help, I can’t say that is something I do easily but I think with time I am getting more relaxed in sharing my frustrations.
